ACL rehab depends heavily on:
whether the tear is partial (Grade 1–2) or complete (Grade 3),
whether surgery was done or planned,
swelling/pain levels,
knee stability,
and clearance from a physician or physical therapist.
A Grade 3 ACL tear especially should be evaluated by an orthopedic specialist before starting aggressive rehab. The exercises below are general PT-style exercises commonly used in conservative rehab and early recovery.

Grade 1 ACL Tear (Mild Stretching of Ligament)
Goals
Reduce swelling/pain
Restore range of motion
Maintain strength
Return to activity gradually
Early Exercises
1. Heel Slides
Improve knee flexion.
Lie on your back
Slide heel toward buttocks
Slowly straighten
Reps: 10–15
Sets: 2–3

2. Quad Sets
Activate quadriceps.
Straighten leg
Tighten thigh muscle
Push knee downward into towel/floor
Hold 5–10 seconds.
Reps: 10–15

3. Straight Leg Raises
Strengthen quads without knee stress.
Keep knee straight
Lift leg ~12 inches
Lower slowly
Reps: 10–15
Sets: 2–3

4. Standing Hamstring Curls
Bend knee slowly
Bring heel toward butt
Reps: 10–15

5. Calf Raises
Rise onto toes
Lower slowly
Reps: 15–20

6. Single-Leg Balance
Stand on injured leg
Hold 20–30 seconds
Progress to unstable surfaces later.

Grade 2 ACL Tear (Partial Tear)
Goals
Restore stability
Strengthen surrounding muscles
Avoid pivoting/twisting early
Exercises
Continue Grade 1 exercises PLUS:

1. Mini Squats
Only partial depth initially.
Feet shoulder-width apart
Squat to ~45 degrees
Keep knees aligned
Reps: 10–15

2. Step-Ups
Use low step
Step up slowly
Control descent
Reps: 10 each leg

3. Bridges
Strengthen glutes/hamstrings.
Lie on back
Knees bent
Lift hips upward
Hold 2–3 seconds.
Reps: 10–15

4. Resistance Band Terminal Knee Extensions
Band behind knee
Slight bend → straighten knee
Reps: 15

5. Side-Lying Leg Raises
Hip stability work.
Reps: 10–15

6. Stationary Bike
Low resistance.
Start 5–10 minutes.

Grade 3 ACL Tear (Complete Tear)
This often requires structured PT and sometimes surgery, especially for athletes or unstable knees.
Main Rehab Goals
Restore motion
Reduce swelling
Strengthen quads/hamstrings/glutes
Improve stability
Protect knee from instability
Avoid:
Pivoting
Cutting movements
Jumping early
Deep twisting
Early Phase Exercises
1. Quad Sets
Critical after complete tear.
2. Heel Slides
3. Straight Leg Raises
4. Ankle Pumps
Reduce swelling and improve circulation.

Intermediate Exercises
5. Wall Sits
Partial range initially.
Hold 15–30 seconds.

6. Leg Press (Light Resistance)
Usually shallow range (0–60°).

7. Hamstring Curls
Important dynamic knee support.

8. Single-Leg Balance Progressions
Foam pad
Eyes closed
Reach tasks

9. Clamshells
Hip/glute stabilization.

10. Controlled Lunges
Only when pain/swelling controlled.

Important Rehab Principles
Reduce Swelling First
Use:
Ice
Compression
Elevation
Relative rest
Restore Full Extension Early
Getting the knee fully straight is extremely important.
Avoid Aggressive Open-Chain Knee Extensions Early
Heavy seated knee extensions can increase ACL strain.
Focus on:
Quadriceps
Hamstrings
Glutes
Hip stability
Balance/proprioception

Warning Signs to Stop Exercise
Stop and contact a professional if you get:
Knee buckling/giving out
Sharp pain
Major swelling increase
Locking/catching
Loss of motion
Instability

Best Exercises Overall for ACL Rehab
The most evidence-supported ACL rehab exercises are:
Quad sets
Straight leg raises
Closed-chain squats
Step-ups
Bridges
Hamstring strengthening
Balance/proprioception drills
Glute strengthening
Stationary biking
Progressive single-leg work
